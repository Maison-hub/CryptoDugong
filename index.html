About the Dugong ...

The dugong is an exclusively herbivorous marine mammal. In adulthood it can reach a height of 3 meters long for a weight of 450 kg. The main habitat areas of the dugong range from east coast of Africa to new caledonia passing by the northern coasts of Australia. In 2019, 40,000 dugongs were recorded worldwide, which makes a species in danger of extinction.

Why the dugong is vulnerable ?

- It is hunted for its meat. Main reason for its disappearance in certain regions. Despite the ban on fishing it like in New Caledonia, poachers continue to hunt it.
- The dugong is present in areas suitable for fishing, which is why it is often found accidentally in the nets of fishermen.
- The dugong feeds mainly on seagrass beds. This ecosystem is fragile and is threatened by pollution and global warming. The degradation of seagrass beds therefore directly impacts dugong populations.
- Finally, near the coast, the marine traffic is dense, so the dugong finds himself regularly hit or cut by the propellers of the boats.
